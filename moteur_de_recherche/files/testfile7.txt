Chile: The U.S. government's covert support for the coup that ousted President Salvador Allende in 1973 and installed General Augusto Pinochet's dictatorship has long been a source of controversy. The Pinochet regime's brutal repression of dissent, including torture, disappearances, and extrajudicial killings, stained U.S.-Chile relations and raised questions about Washington's commitment to democracy and human rights.
